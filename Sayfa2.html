The role of MHP, USA and Nato to build Ülkücü movement

Around the end of 60's there was a huge leftist movement happening in Turkey. Due to previous regime by Adnan Menderes, Turkey was turning into a source for USA. In Menderes time, he closed schools in small towns, in order to get more vote from people, he even quotes "if we educate them they will not vote for us". He was doing the bidding of USA, cancelling train rails projects, burning opium farms which they were used for pharmacy and accepting Marshall pact which this pact led to an inner govermental organization called "Counter-Guerrilla" which was the turkish branch of NATO's stay-behind operation in case of a "soviet invasion". In Italy it was called Gladio....

Adnan Menderes's time ended with 1960 coup but the deep state structure was still there. In 1965, Justice party came to power which was the continuation of Menderes's party. In this period, there was a huge leftist movement happening in Turkey, especially in universities. The leftist movement was not only in universities but also in working class neighbourhoods. The key event that led to Ülkücü movement and Commando camps was 1969 6th fleet protests in Istanbul. The protest was against USA's 6th fleet visiting Istanbul, the protest was organised by students and working class people....

During the protest, a group of students from Istanbul University were marching towards American consulate when they were attacked by a group of people armed with knives and sticks. The attack was organised by a group called "Turkish Hearths" which is the predecessor of Ülkücü movement. During the attack, 2 students were killed and many were injured. After the attack, leftist movement in Turkey started to gain more momentum, especially in universities. In response to leftist movement, Ülkücü movement started to gain more momentum as well. They started to organise themselves in commando camps, where they were trained in guerrilla warfare. The camps were established in various parts of Turkey, especially in Central Anatolia and Eastern Anatolia. The United States funded and supplied weapons to the camps, where the instructors were Turkish irregulars previously trained by the US...

By the late 60s, the MHP ("Milliyetçi" Movement Party) began executing a National Socialist (Nazi) doctrine, using "Turkish Nationalism" as a mask to dismantle the democratic order. They established "Commando Camps"—paramilitary training grounds modeled directly after Hitler’s SS and SA Storm Units—to breed a "striking force" to crush revolutionary movements. Recruits endured a brutal 03:30 AM regime of combat and weapons training designed to prepare them for seizing power and "cleansing" minorities within a racially pure "Turan" empire. During the Maraş Massacre (1978), Ülkücü militants, organized under the "National Socialist" doctrine mentioned in official 1970 reports. In cities like Maraş and Çorum, these groups ignited massacres by marking the houses of Non-Muslim Minorities, Kurds, Alevis and Leftists, inciting mob violence, and conducting systematic killings that resulted in hundreds of deaths. Their actions were not mere street clashes but were calculated "striking force" operations aimed at creating enough chaos to justify a military takeover and the eventual establishment of a racially unified "Turan" empire.
